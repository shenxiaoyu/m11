PROJECT INTRODUCTION

RESEARCH QUESTION

What would an indication signal be for Asian cyclers on Dutch cycle track?

 

BACKGROUND

Hand signal is one of the most common bicycle languages in Netherlands, which helps avoid accidents effectively. Netherlands is a bike kingdom, hand signal undeniably becomes a social identity for Dutch people. On the other hand, people from Asian were found hard to get used to this traffic language. China is one of the typical countries where cyclers used to ride in crowd and follow the traffic lights to turn instead of using hand signal. This cycling habit promotes the formation of a totally different social cycling identity. This research starts from investigating the reasons behind the phenomenon of different frequency of hand signals use and attempts to generate knowledge on how interactive design can help Asian people to express their own cycling language in hand signal countries.

 

ACTIVITIES

The "Research Through Design" framework is adopted as the scheme to guide the research process. The research process went through five main phases: Research Direction, Exploration, Analysis, Synthesis and Evaluation. Observation and interview were adopted to get a close approach to the context and users. In the interview, 7 Asian and 4 Dutch cyclers were invited to share their cycling experiences. Then AEIOU analysis and affinity diagramming were used to make a selection of useful information and gain insights from the stage of Exploration. Based on a co-creation of 6 designers, four kinds of manipulation were developed as main interventions  to collect quantitative and qualitative data based on users’ requirements on cycling signals interaction. After the user test, it was found that the simplest and most traditional manipulator, which requires less memory load and have natural interaction, performed best.

As mentioned in the Learning Approach, I started to transfer my role of designer into a design researcher with a research module and several guiding books. During the process, different methods and various ways of analysis is applied, AEIOU analysis, affinity diagramming, Likert scale, etc. By going through an one-semester project, I have experienced several basic research steps. Besides, it is also crucial to select proper methods to meet the goal of certain stage. In that case, my coach gave me a lot help to get into the right direction.

 

This is a user-centered research project. People were involved in almost every important phases. In order to gain deep insights from users, I learned how to ask proper questions. Words full of suggestions are not appropriate for a research interview. Give an open-ended question and see how people react naturally is a better choice. On the other hand, I improved my observation skills by using AEIOU framework, which systematically give five keywords for observers: activities, environments, interactions, objects and users. I would like to adopt this framework to collect and organize information in my future design process.

 

As my project was connected close to cultural differences, I studied the differences of cycling experience between Asian and European. Three perspectives were adopted to gain a full picture. Through this research, I have a deeper understanding of the reasons behind certain phenomenon which could not be judged from the surface. Besides, my research also help to promote the communication between cyclers from Asian and European to develop empathy with each other.

MODULE BACKGROUND

The main goal of this module is to investigate differentiation in tradition and culture as the basis to come to innovative designs that resonate with our globalising market. Using methods such as taking first/ second/ third perspectives to investigate differences in cultural behaviors, I learned to abstract the deeper meaning behind and apply this in the development of a intercultural design. The assignment chosen in this module is to design a new way of serving Dutch dairy food to discover fundamental ‘forgotten if not unrealized' values behind it. I chose this module with expectation to experience a different design process and gain a better understanding of culture differentiation.

During the process, I cooperated with students from different culture background. We explored the design opportunities with different perspectives. At last, we designed a DIY dairy desert by taking the inspiration of the unique Dutch package culture. The main idea is to encourage people to get close to foods and think about nature of them.

MODULE BACKGROUND

This module aimed at exploring the notion of ritual and its qualities through experience and designing. Through the design of a ritual (welcoming guests with tea), we explored the characteristics of designing a ritual and its artifacts, and the relations existing between artifacts, gestures, events meanings, and values. The main intention to choose this module is to explore the conversation between human and artifacts and its value for interaction design. Like Japanese tea ceremony we experienced at the beginning, mature rituals are the mirror of our design. But the point is what we can learn from that, and how far we can go against the static routines. It was a challenge for 17 people to work on one ritual only, great internal communication was required in this module. Through the process of value exploration, synthesis, iterative prototyping, at last, we designed a welcoming tea ceremony and artifacts to supporting it.

MAJOR DEVELOPMENT

Values of the conversation between acts and artifacts

Iterative prototyping

for essential details

Cooperation and communication

This module led me to a new vision of interaction design and service design. The conversation between acts and artifacts add to the value of series of acts. When the black tea merged into water and created a nice cloud, the candle holders transferred the heat to the tea, they all expressed the care and hospitality to guests in silence. Values weigh more than the taste of tea. When I design something in the future, it will remind me that not only focus on “tea” itself, but also think about how to add value to the process and make it a meaningful experience.

Iterative prototyping is one of the most important design process here. We improved the resolution of prototypes step by step, which is helpful for people to share ideas and discuss about the design. The challenge here is how to avoid “very complete” deliverables but put more emphasis on the essential aspects or details in the design which elicits beauty in the experience.

Although we had a distribution of task, it was still a challenge for 17 designers to work on one ritual. The communication became extremely important to find consensus. People held strong ideas, in the meanwhile, we were flexible to accept different ideas and had faith in each other. But things didn’t go so smoothly all the time. In the table team, we have some misunderstanding because of different cultural backgrounds. But we had a good conversation and everything is fine again. As an international student, It was an important lesson to me. By the way, the communication is always the best way to solve problems. I am glad that I did so as soon as I noticed the problem.
