WHERE I STARTED...

In my past learning experience, I was developing design skills by working on all kinds of projects, ranging from graphic design, interactive design, web design, product design to business model design. During my bachelor education, I focused more on the deliverables themselves rather than my own development as a designer. Although I did improve my skills of design to certain degree, I was proceeding aimlessly on the road towards a qualified designer.

When I started my master program in TU/e, I decided to re-organize my development of capabilities to have an overview of myself as a designer. By writing Personal Development Plan, I learned both my strengths and weaknesses. In order to be a rounded designer, several competency areas would be chosen to be the main abilities to improve.

EXPECTATION OF THIS SEMESTER

Adapt to the new environment.

As this is the very first semester of my overseas study, I want to take it as an adaptive period for both study and life. It is exciting and challenging to study in a foreign country. The biggest difficulties may be the adaption of language and culture. I believe communication with people will help a lot. On the other hand, I need to get used to the new education system as soon as possible. Learn how it works and get started with my development as a master student.

Develop research skills

and the ability of prototyping.

Now I am involved in a research program of wearable senses, which requires good research skills and the abilities of prototyping. Research can bring new knowledge and insights which are valuable to design. As for prototyping, in the stage of the bachelor, I focused on the on-screen interaction design, so I didn’t have many chances to make physical prototypes by myself. Prototypes is a way of thinking, which can give a direct and instant feedback to the initial concept. It is one of the most important skills for a designer.

Identity and responsibility

of a qualified designer.

In the stage of bachelor education, design is an interest, a set of courses, an experiment, a life style for me. It is not satisfactory any more to purely strengthen abilities in my master program. I need to develop my own vision on the world around me.

As a junior employee in the department of Industrial Design in TU/e, it’s time to regard myself as a designer and think more about the identity and responsibility as a qualified designer.

REASONS

People are the stakeholder in design process.

It is important for an interaction designer to understand the cultural background of users. In order to create culturally appropriate systems, products and related services,I should understand how people’s mind sets are based on their values, beliefs, behavior and ontological assumptions, and how to put these into design practice. Social-cultural awareness will bring the unique opportunity to explore and create something valuable.

LONG-TERM EXPECTATION

 

Thanks to the 3+2 Excellence program between Technology University of Eindhoven and Zhejiang University, I have the chance to spend 2 years studying and living in Netherlands. I want to develop myself as a designer and even more importantly as a person. As a master student of Industrial design, I will not only focus on the deliverables of projects, but put more emphasis on the exploration of my own vision towards design. Studying abroad provides me the precious opportunity to experience different cultures and broaden my horizon with more experimental European design. On the other hand, I want to build up my independent personality as a person in these two years. Learn how to adapt to new environments and communicate or cooperate with people from different cultural backgrounds.
